Domestic Violence

Domestic Violence is using physical force that hurts someone in our household, family, or in a relationship with us. It includes the following:

  • Pushing or shoving
  • Grabbing to restrict movement
  • Slapping
  • Kicking
  • Biting
  • Hitting with a fist or object
  • Using a knife, firearm, or other weapon to inflict harm

Yes, this is called emotional abuse and can come in many forms. The following are some examples of emotional abuse:

  • Controlling your partner’s time, space, money, thoughts, or choices such as what they wear
  • Monitoring where your partner goes or what they spend money on
  • Isolating your partner by not letting them see or talk to others
  • Accusing your partner of flirting, having an affair, or being unfaithful when there is little or no evidence they have done so
  • Intimidating your partner by making them afraid, including breaking things, punching walls, slamming doors, or throwing objects
  • Threatening to hurt your partner, their children, their pets, or damage their property, even if you don’t follow through on the threat
  • Threatening to hurt yourself, especially when things are not going your way
  • Threatening to leave or divorce your partner, threatening to not let them see their children
  • Demeaning your partner with frequent put-downs, name calling, blame, or humiliation
  • Saying things that are designed to make your partner feel “crazy” or “stupid”
  • Punishing your partner by refusing to talk to them or by withholding affection

If after reading these lists, you see yourself in the descriptions, you aren't alone. Like me, you may not intend to or want to do these things, but end up doing them anyway. You don't have to live like that forever, you can change. Make it your goal to never again use physical force in a conflict to get your way, and to minimize your emotionally abusive behavior. Stopping the physical abuse may keep you out of jail, but stopping the emotional abuse as well will help ensure you build healthy, satisfying relationships and a happy life. Michael Clark and his foundation, the Ananais Foundation, helped me immensely and can help you too if you're ready to stop the cycle. You can get in touch with them here.
