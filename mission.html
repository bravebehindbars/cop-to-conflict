The Mission

I hope to be able to achieve several things in the areas how our system treats folks with mental health struggles (veterans in particular), dealing with the issue of domestic violence in our society, and further criminal justice reform. Unfortunately, being incarcerated makes it extremely difficult to do much of anything while here. Upon being released, I hope to be able to use my unique perspective, experience, and connections I've made through my career and incarceration to start pushing for reform to the system as a whole. I also hope to be able to help other people who may be going down the same road I was prior to my incarceration, and help steer them off that path before they hurt themselves, a loved one, or end up incarcerated themselves.

Veterans, PTSD, and Incarceration

A portion of society seems to have a view of veterans with PTSD as violent, unhinged animals just waiting to snap. I believe this view couldn't be further from the truth, and locking our veterans up and throwing away the key isn't the answer to dealing with our veterans suffering from PTSD.

Domestic Violence

Victims need to get justice for what was done to them, and be able to feel safe moving forward in life. There is no excuse or justifiable reason for domestic violence. Abusers need to get help in order to change their behavior and stop hurting the people they love. I believe a more compassionate approach to dealing with perpetrators or domestic violence is needed to change that behavior.

More on Domestic Violence
Further Criminal Justice Reform

As someone who went from locking people up to being locked up, I believe my perspective is a rare and unique one, and could help facilitate a lot of positive change in our system. I hope this change will benefit victims of crime, as well as provide a more fair process and system for the accused.
